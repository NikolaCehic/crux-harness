High conversion costs, financing gaps, and fiscal leakage can make conversion subsidies weaker than alternative housing interventions on a per-unit public cost basis.

A subsidy is more defensible when it unlocks units that would not otherwise happen and includes affordability, speed, and additionality requirements.

The policy decision should compare conversion subsidies against zoning reform, direct affordable housing support, and permitting improvements.
