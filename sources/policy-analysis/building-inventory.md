Office-to-residential conversions can add housing supply only when a meaningful share of vacant buildings is physically suitable and financially feasible.

Suitability depends on floor plate depth, window access, plumbing conversion cost, structural constraints, code compliance, and neighborhood demand.

The city should inventory representative buildings before allocating broad subsidy funds.
