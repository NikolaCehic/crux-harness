Gartner's 2025 survey of IT application leaders reports that many organizations are piloting or deploying some form of AI agents, but only 15 percent are considering, piloting, or deploying fully autonomous AI agents.

The survey highlights governance, maturity, and agent sprawl as concerns that hamper deployment of truly agentic systems.

Implication for Crux: the strongest enterprise wedge is unlikely to be raw autonomy alone. Governance, maturity, scope control, and deployment readiness are central adoption constraints.
